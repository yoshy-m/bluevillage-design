About Wild Nature Camp

The snow has melted away and Hokkaido is in the season of fresh green leaves. We will hold a primitive camp that touches the most fundamental life of us human beings.

Completely removed from modern technology such as cell phones and computers. We make a fire with flint, eat simple meals, and sleep on beds made of certain things.

During the camp, we will also do some introspection work based on Native American wisdom. You will connect deeply with your inner self and share it with your fellow campers, creating new bonds.

For those who are seeking a deeper connection with nature, for those who are at a turning point in their lives, for those who are seeking a deeper connection with nature, for those who are seeking a deeper connection with nature.
If you are looking for a real sense of life, spend 3 days and 2 nights at this camp. We hope you will gain a new vision of life and a new sense of "living".

Greetings

Message from New Day Douglas, Organizer

Greetings from the forest and lakeshore of Toyako!
I’m Doug, and we at NewDay and Blue Village are passionate about giving everyone an unforgettable experience of a lifetime. Please don’t miss this opportunity with us to discover Nature with new eyes, on an inner journey of growth in awareness, belonging, and connection. Our aim is to truly enjoy this time with an energetic, relaxing, and transformative approach. Every aspect of this unique camp has been created and improved with over 40 years of experience in the wild, to give us a fresh perspective of Nature and ourselves thru an immersion in simplicity and gratitude. We’re likely to be more challenged mentally than physically, so this amount of time in such beauty with our new friends becoming like family is guaranteed to be very empowering. These gifts will also flow into our inner strength and confidence which impacts all areas of our lives. There are more risks in daily city life, however all precautions will be implemented to ensure our safety.
Just as we will share our many stories together, please feel free to contact us anytime with any questions or concerns. Thanks and see you here!
"May the Great Mystery make sunrise in your heart." -Sioux prayer

Message from Blue Village Gosuke, Co-Organizer

I met Doug last winter. I attended a workshop he organized for adults, where we played like kids in the forest and he taught us how to make a "debris hut," a simple dwelling made of only dead leaves and twigs.
By some fortunate coincidence, we lived very close to each other, and we began to talk about many things over a beer or dinner together. This camp project is the realization of one of the visions that emerged from these moments at the dinner table. Doug and I are about the same age as father and son, but I think we share one underlying wish. We want the earth to remain beautiful and blue, and we want to do what we can to support it. I am very happy to be able to work with him in creating this camp, and I am looking forward to seeing what kind of time we will have together.

Gosuke Uno(Camp Co Organizer)

Born in 1988. Born in New York City, raised in Kansai, Japan, and a resident of Blue Village. Hypnotherapist for Inner Journey Travel Agency, studied abroad in the Blue Mountains of Australia at the age of 16. He found it interesting to be exposed to different cultures and worlds, and went on to study at the School of International Liberal Studies at Waseda University. As a student, He was involved in educational NPO activities in Cambodia.
After graduation, he was searching for his life mission while developing a wide range of activities such as managing a tutoring school, planning children's camps, and musical activities. He traveled to more than 40 countries, including Mongolia, New Zealand, and Peru, and continued to explore his inner world by participating in various programs, and the vision for Blue Village came down to him when he first came to Lake Toya in 2016. He then found a piece of land with a water spring in the forest by Lake Toya and started building a hut with his friends; in early 2020, he sold his business in Tokyo and moved completely to Lake Toya.
Currently working as a hypnotherapist, a guide to the inner world, and creating a forest where people can connect with their soul's desire and express it freely.
